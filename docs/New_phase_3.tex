\section*{Owner--Imitator Training with POU Readout}

\subsection*{Geometry and readout}
The tiles $\{\Omega_j\}_{j\in\mathcal L}$ are the regions of the leaves
$\mathcal L$ of the full binary decision tree; as leaves of a full
binary subdivision they tile $\Omega$. Every point $X \in \Omega$
therefore has a unique \emph{owner}
\begin{equation}
k(X) \in \mathcal L
\quad\text{defined by}\quad
X \in \Omega_{k(X)} .
\end{equation}
Each expert $u_j$ carries a smoothstep window $\Psi_j$ supported on the
inflated box $\widetilde\Omega_j \supset \Omega_j$; the fat band
$\widetilde\Omega_j \setminus \Omega_j$ (width $\delta$) is its
\emph{collar}, on which $j$ is never the owner. The global model is the
normalized POU composition, used as \emph{readout only} --- no loss term
is ever evaluated on it:
\begin{equation}
u_\theta(X) \;=\; \sum_{j\in\mathcal L} \tilde\Psi_j(X)\, u_j(X),
\qquad
\tilde\Psi_j = \frac{\Psi_j}{\sum_{i\in\mathcal L}\Psi_i}.
\label{eq:comp}
\end{equation}

\subsection*{Loss}
Every expert has exactly one role at every point: \emph{owner}
(physics) on its tile, \emph{imitator} (distillation) elsewhere. The
loss of this stage is, for each expert $j$,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L_j
= \mathcal L^{res}_j + \mathcal L^{ic}_j + \mathcal L^{bc}_j
+ \mathcal L^{per}_j + \mathcal L^{imit}_j ,
\label{eq:loss}
\end{equation}
with each term a mean over a sample set drawn from its region:
\begin{align}
\mathcal L^{res}_j
&= \frac{1}{|X^{res}_j|} \sum_{X \in X^{res}_j}
   \bigl\| \mathcal N[u_j](X) \bigr\|^2 ,
&& X^{res}_j \subset \Omega_j ,
\label{eq:term_res}\\
\mathcal L^{ic}_j
&= \frac{1}{|X^{ic}_j|} \sum_{X \in X^{ic}_j}
   \bigl\| u_j(X) - g_{ic}(X) \bigr\|^2 ,
&& X^{ic}_j \subset \Omega_j \cap \{t = 0\} ,
\label{eq:term_ic}\\
\mathcal L^{bc}_j
&= \frac{1}{|X^{bc}_j|} \sum_{X \in X^{bc}_j}
   \bigl\| \mathcal B[u_j](X) - g_{bc}(X) \bigr\|^2 ,
&& X^{bc}_j \subset \partial\Omega_j \cap \partial\Omega_{non\text{-}per} ,
\label{eq:term_bc}\\
\mathcal L^{per}_j
&= \frac{1}{|X^{per}_j|} \sum_{X \in X^{per}_j}
   \sum_{\alpha \in \mathcal A_{per}}
   \bigl\| D^\alpha u_j(X) - D^\alpha u^\star_\lambda(\sigma(X))
   \bigr\|^2 ,
&& X^{per}_j \subset \partial\Omega_j \cap \partial\Omega_{per} ,
\label{eq:term_per}\\
\mathcal L^{imit}_j
&= \frac{1}{|X^{imit}_j|} \sum_{X \in X^{imit}_j}
   \sum_{|\alpha| \le m-1} w_\alpha
   \bigl\| D^\alpha u_j(X) - D^\alpha u^\star_\lambda(X) \bigr\|^2 ,
&& X^{imit}_j \subset \widetilde\Omega_j \setminus \Omega_j .
\label{eq:term_imit}
\end{align}
Here $g_{ic}, g_{bc}$ are the given initial/boundary data,
$\mathcal B$ the (Dirichlet or Neumann) boundary operator,
$\sigma(X)$ the mirror map of the periodic boundary
(e.g.\ $\sigma(x_{\min},t) = (x_{\max},t)$),
$\mathcal A_{per}$ the derivative set of the global periodic condition,
and $m$ the PDE order (set in config): both minted terms match values
\emph{and derivatives up to order $m-1$}. The IC/BC/periodic terms apply
only to experts whose tile meets the corresponding portion of
$\partial\Omega$. The total loss is
$\sum_{j\in\mathcal L} \mathcal L_j$; the summands are mutually
independent, so all experts train in parallel under one optimizer.

\subsection*{Minted target}
Both minted terms use the same homotopy,
\begin{equation}
u^\star_\lambda(X)
= \lambda\, u_0(X)
+ (1-\lambda)\;\sg\!\bigl[\, u_{k(X)}(X) \,\bigr],
\label{eq:mint}
\end{equation}
where $k(X)$ is the owner --- the tile owner on the collar, the mirror
expert on the periodic boundary --- and $\sg[\cdot]$ is a stop-gradient
snapshot: targets (values and derivatives, computed by autodiff through
the frozen snapshot) are constant data between refreshes and never move
with the weights. $\lambda$ decreases linearly from $1$ to a
configurable floor $\lambda_{\min} \in [0,1]$ over the refresh events:
$\lambda_{\min} = 1$ is pure root anchoring; $\lambda_{\min} = 0$ is the
full exchange in which each imitator copies the current owner.

\subsection*{Why the smooth readout inherits the hard accuracy}
Wherever imitation is tight, the blend \emph{degenerates} to the owner:
at a seam, $u_\theta = \tilde\Psi_j u_j + \tilde\Psi_k u_k \to u_k$
whenever $u_j \to u_k$ on the overlap, independently of the window
values. The POU never averages two different solutions (no
nonlinear-superposition issue --- locally it represents a single
function); it returns the piecewise-owner solution, $C^N$-smooth by
construction. The blend's residual excess at a seam is bounded by
$\sum_{i=1}^{m} \varepsilon_{m-i}\,\delta^{-i}$, with $\varepsilon_p$
the imitation error in the $p$-th derivative --- which is why the
minted terms match derivatives up to $m-1$: value-only imitation would
leave the $\varepsilon_0\,\delta^{-m}$ term, fatal for high-order PDEs.

\subsection*{Training}
One optimizer over the summed loss \eqref{eq:loss}, switching from Adam
to SSBroyden with Strong Wolfe line search. Every $R$ epochs
(\texttt{resample\_every}), three things happen together:
\begin{enumerate}
\item residual, collar, and boundary points are resampled;
\item the snapshots of all owners are updated to the current weights,
and all minted targets \eqref{eq:mint} are re-evaluated;
\item $\lambda$ takes one linear step toward $\lambda_{\min}$.
\end{enumerate}
Between refreshes the loss landscape is fixed. The interaction with the
optimizers follows established practice for quasi-Newton PINN training.
Random resampling benefits first-order methods but is known to be
incompatible with running full-batch quasi-Newton updates, which rely on
past trajectories for their curvature estimates; the standard remedies
in the SSBroyden/L-BFGS literature are either (i) a single fixed batch
for the entire quasi-Newton phase, or (ii) a two-loop structure in which
an outer loop resamples the points and each inner loop is a
deterministic solve on the resulting fixed batch. Our refresh loop is
exactly the two-loop pattern, with the outer iteration additionally
updating the snapshots and $\lambda$. Hence:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Every refresh restarts the SSBroyden state} (the inner
solve begins with empty quasi-Newton memory on the new deterministic
landscape); Adam state may simply continue. $R$ must therefore be large
enough for each inner solve to be productive --- on the order of several
thousand iterations during the SSBroyden phase --- while during the Adam
phase $R$ may be small.
\item \textbf{Refreshing from partially converged owners is inexact
(Jacobi) Schwarz} --- convergent, but with very small $R$ the minted
targets carry transient noise; the same lower bound on $R$ covers this.
\end{itemize}
The refresh loop is the Schwarz mechanism in disguise: an owner's
weakest region is the rim of its own tile, and the data it exports to
its neighbors' collars comes from that rim; at each refresh the roles
reverse, and each expert's accurate interior supplies the neighbor's
rim. The first phase ($\lambda = 1$) anchors everyone to the root;
successive refreshes let seam quality contract below the root's error.

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{Owner--imitator training of AToE experts (fine-tune excluded)}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require converged root $u_0$; tiles $\{\Omega_j\}$ (tree leaves),
windows $\{\Psi_j\}$ (collar width $\delta$); PDE order $m$; epochs $E$,
\texttt{resample\_every} $R$; $\lambda$ schedule $1 \to \lambda_{\min}$
(linear over refreshes); Adam$\to$SSBroyden switch point
\State Initialize experts; per-leaf input normalization
\State Sample all point sets; snapshot all parameters; mint targets
(values $+$ derivatives up to $m-1$) at $\lambda = 1$
\Comment{$\equiv$ mint to $u_0$}
\For{epoch $= 1,\dots,E$} \Comment{one optimizer, all experts in
parallel}
  \State step on $\sum_j \mathcal L_j$ \eqref{eq:loss}
  (Adam, then SSBroyden after the switch)
  \If{epoch $\bmod R = 0$}
    \State resample points; snapshot all parameters; step $\lambda$;
    re-mint all targets \eqref{eq:mint}
    \State reset SSBroyden memory (Adam state may persist)
  \EndIf
\EndFor
\Ensure smooth readout $u_\theta$ \eqref{eq:comp}, collapsing to the
piecewise-owner solution wherever imitation is tight
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
